01 — The problem

Beautiful screens that didn't add up.

We had a library of polished prototypes — a hub, a grid, a canvas, a live view. Each looked sharp in isolation. But click from one to the next and the illusion broke: different chrome, different logic, a jolt at every seam. In a review, a senior leader put it bluntly.

It's like walking down a muddy street. Nice tiles, then a puddle, then more tiles. You never trust the next step.
Senior leadership, design review

That reframed the entire brief. The problem wasn't the quality of any one screen — it was the continuity between them. So I changed the unit of design from the page to the journey: one flow, walked exactly as a Demand Manager would, with no puddles allowed.

02 — The persona

I treated a “day in the life” doc as the spec.

Before pushing a single pixel, the journey was written out line by line — the Demand Manager's first sign-in, Tango's first hello, the moment they see what needs attention, the demand they open, the action they take. That narrative became the holy grail: every screen had to answer a specific moment in it.

Where a moment needed a capability we don't have yet, it was marked in red and left honest — never faked for the demo. Designing against a real story is what kept the journey coherent instead of a tour of features.

03 — The journey, running live

Scroll the story. The real product moves with you — then take the controls.

STEP 01 — Arrival

A conversational front door

No empty dashboard. The Demand Manager lands in a calm, conversational space where Tango introduces itself and offers to walk them in. The first thirty seconds are designed to feel like being met, not onboarded.

Onboarding · Conversational UI
STEP 02 — What needs me

The demand grid, sorted by attention

Instead of a wall of rows, the grid leads with what's slipping, what's mine, and what's next. A lean default set of columns, with an intuitive “Manage Columns” control so power users can opt into density — not drown in it.

Data-dense tables · Progressive disclosure
STEP 03 — The snapshot

Work, insight & action in one drawer

Click a demand and a snapshot slides in: a compact stage pill that expands on hover, a priority level-meter, “your move” for leads, a Tango insight, and the pending actions — the unified view leadership asked for, without leaving the grid.

Information hierarchy · Side drawer
STEP 04 — Go deep

Into the demand canvas

When the moment calls for depth, the same demand opens as a canvas: overview and success criteria, strategy and key results, RAID risks with mitigation, a RACI of stakeholders, financials, and an SLA-aware journey — all one click from the grid.

IA · RAID / RACI · Financial viz

“Let Tango do it”

The journey closes the loop: from any action the Manager can hand work to Tango — draft the user stories, move the item, schedule the follow-up. Agentic help framed as delegation, with the human always in the lead.

04 — Designing Tango

An AI chief-of-staff has to earn trust, not just attention.

Tango is the hardest design problem in the product: an agent that can act on enterprise work. Three principles kept it feeling like a trusted colleague rather than a black box.

Work comes to you

Tango's job is to surface the right demand, milestone, or action at the right moment — turning a passive tool into one that brings the work, sorted by what actually needs a human.

Always show your reasoning

Every Tango insight is paired with the “why” — the signal it noticed. An AI-gradient language marks exactly where Tango is thinking, so suggestions read as transparent, not magical.

The human stays in the lead

Tango proposes and can execute, but every agentic action is framed as delegation the Manager chooses — “let Tango do it” — never an automation that happens to them.

05 — The invisible seam

A design decision that's really an engineering one.

The journey stitches two large prototypes — the onboarding landing and the workspace — that were never built to live together. Inline-merging them meant colliding CSS and JavaScript: both defined the same class names and helper functions. The clean fix was architectural.

Embedded as an iframe — fully preserved, zero collisions with the workspace it lives inside.
postMessagehandoff
The shell
Workspace app
Listens for the handoff, fades the landing out, and routes straight into the demand grid — a transition that feels native.

The result: the user crosses from a sign-in into a complex enterprise app and feels one product, not two prototypes taped together. The seam is invisible — which is exactly the point of the whole project.

06 — Where it landed

From a critique to a journey that holds.

The muddy street is paved. What was a set of admired-but-disconnected screens is now a single walkable journey used to present the product to leadership — and the method (story-as-spec, continuity over polish, invisible seams) now sets the bar for everything that follows.
